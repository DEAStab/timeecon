The dead time

Agentic coding gave you your hours back — sitting down.

You write a prompt and the agent works for three, six, twelve minutes. So you wait. You watch the spinner, or you reach for another tab. Multiply that across a day and it's hours of new, sedentary in-between time — the cost of the machine's productivity, quietly paid by your body.

Time Economy treats that in-between as the most reclaimable part of your day. Not rest, not distraction — movement, sized precisely to how long your agent will run.

How it works

It turns every agent run into a reason to move.

Three things happen automatically, in the gap you used to waste — no timers to set, nothing to remember.

🔌
Step 01

Hooks into the loop

It plugs in between your agent's prompting steps. The instant a run starts, Time Economy knows — no buttons, no babysitting.

🚶
Step 02

Sizes the break to the run

A quick four-minute run earns a stretch at your desk; a twelve-minute migration earns a real walk. The movement always fits the window.

↩️
Step 03

Brings you back on time

It returns you to the keyboard the second your agent needs a review or the next prompt — so moving never costs you a beat of progress.

🌿

Movement, not notifications

Calm, embodied prompts — mobility, posture, a short walk. No buzzing badges, no guilt, no leaderboard to keep.

🎯

Respects deep work

When you're truly in flow, it stays quiet. It fills the dead time between runs; it never interrupts the live work.

📈

Kind, quiet tracking

Active minutes and standing time per ship, shown gently. Progress you can feel — not a streak you can break.

The product

A thin layer between your prompts — and your chair.

Time Economy lives inside the agent workflow you already have. It watches the run, not you.

01

Sits in the agent loop

Plugs in between prompting steps in the agents you already use, reading only when a run begins and ends. Your code never leaves your machine.

02

Private by design

No repository access, no telemetry on your work. It needs the rhythm of your runs — not their contents — to do its job.

03

Built by engineers and physios

Movement designed for desk bodies — wrists, neck, hips, posture — by people who understand both the codebase and the spine.

04

Yours to tune

Set the intensity, the kinds of movement, and your quiet hours. It adapts to your day — not the other way around.
